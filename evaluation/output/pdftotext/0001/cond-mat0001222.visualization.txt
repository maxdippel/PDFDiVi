[48;5;167m2.7 K and 300 K up to 0.5 THz. The data are well understood in a Drude approach with a negligible temperature dependence of the plasma frequency ωp and scattering time τ with an additional[0m
[48;5;167mcontribution of localized charges. The dielectric properties resemble those of the best ”metallic”[0m

Depending on the wrapping of the graphene sheet, the intrinsic electronic properties of single-wall [90m[[0m[38;5;1mcarbonnanotubes[0m[90m/[0m[38;5;22mcarbon-nanotubes[0m[90m][0m (SWNTs) are either semiconducting (zigzag and most chiral nanotubes) or metallic (armchair and part of the chiral nanotubes) [90m[\cite=Hamada92][\cite=Wildoer98].[0m Single ropes of armchaired SWNTs as well as entangled networks (mats) show a decrease of the dc conductivity, σdc, with increasing temperature (T) for T above a critical temperature (T*[90m[\cite=Fischer97][\cite=Kane98] [\cite=Kaiser98] [\cite=Petit97].[0m For T T*σdc decreases with cooling. Here we apply this method to mats of SWNTs. The data do not need Kramers-Kronig analysis, which is a great advantage for the analysis of the response of the delocalized charge carriers [90m[\cite=Chapman99].[0mLike heavily doped polymers SWNTs are shown to be an example of a system with exceptional long scattering times τ and low plasma frequencies ωp, but in contrast to the polymers metallic islands can be excluded as an explanation. For that reason SWNTs form an important new element in the not-yet understood physics behind τ and ωp.

[38;5;1m‖[0m Highly conducting polymers like doped polyaniline (PAN) and polypyrrole (PPy) [90m[\cite=Lee95][\cite=Kohlman97][0m and SWNT-mats show analogous dielectric behavior. The values of [90m[[0m[38;5;1mǫ′[0m[90m/[0m[38;5;22mε'[0m[90m][0m ω) are strongly frequency and T dependent. [38;5;1m‖[0m In summary, we have shown that the delocalized properties of nanotube mats can be completely determined by sub-THz measurements and are well described in a Drude picture with a negligible temperature dependence of the plasma frequency and scattering time. The values of ωp and τ resemble those found in well conducting doped polymers. When modeled with the usually chosen Fermi velocities, unusually large values of the mean free path result for both systems. This finding and the absence of crystalline regions in the mats underline the need for a better description of these disordered [90m[[0m[38;5;1mstrandlike[0m[90m/[0m[38;5;22mstrand-like[0m[90m][0m systems.
